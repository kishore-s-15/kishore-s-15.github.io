Publications

  • 2024.11.30
    Fairness at Every Intersection: Uncovering and Mitigating Intersectional Biases in Multimodal Clinical Predictions
    Arxiv Pre-print
    This paper addresses the issue of intersectional biases in EHR-based clinical decision-making by examining how multiple demographic attributes interact to create disparate outcomes. The authors benchmark these biases on the MIMIC-Eye1 and MIMIC-IV ED datasets, leveraging pre-trained clinical LMs (MedBERT, Clinical BERT, Clinical BioBERT) to learn unified text representations from multimodal sources. Their proposed sub-group-specific mitigation approach demonstrates robustness across various datasets, subgroups, and embeddings, effectively tackling intersectional biases in complex EHR settings.
